Our Instructors

Suzanne Banbury

- Artistic Director

Mrs Banbury studied extensively in New York City with the American Ballet Theater, Mia Slavenska, Robert Joffrey, Matt Mattox, Martha Graham, Alvin Alley, and David Howard. She performed professionally with the Houston Music Theater.

Mrs Banbury has been teaching in Houston for more than thirty-five years and has had students accepted to the San Francisco Ballet School, the School of American Ballet (NYCB), the Boston School of Ballet, the Milwaukee School of Ballet, Ballet/Aspen, and the Austin Ballet.

Lori Barzilla

- Instructor

Mrs Barzilla began her dance training at the age of eight with the Banbury School of Dance. She danced with the Allegro Civic ballet and went with them to Poland on a five-week dance exchange program. She has also danced with the Discovery Dance Group and Dance Arts Company in Houston.

Mrs Barzilla has studied in New York with Robert Joffrey, Ron Forello, Bob Audy, JoJo, and the American Ballet Theater Ballet School.

She has been teaching at Banbury for nineteen years. Mrs Barzilla has had students accepted to Joffrey Ballet, Alvin Alley, Ballet/Aspen, and the Austin Ballet.

Ashley Cook

- Instructor

A native Houstonian, Ashley began her training at Banbury School of Dance when she was 8 years old and completed her teaching apprenticeship training at 15. After graduation, she continued her dance education at Belhaven College and University of Houston.

She has been fortunate to study several styles of dance including Ballet, Tap, Jazz and Pointe under many instructors including Suzanne Banbury; Kenneth Epting, founder of Exclamation Dance Company; Laura Morton, former instructor at Houston Ballet; Karen Stokes, founder of Travesty Dance Group; Steven and Britta Wynne, founders of At Marah Dance Company and many others. She has been teaching for over ten years and has performed and taught at Banbury School as well as managed dance programs and instructed at Parker Elementary, Dancescape by Joyce, Yellowstone Academy, Spring Creek Athletics and Discover Gymnastics.

Ashley is thrilled, honored and excited to be back at Banbury School of Dance!

Adult Programs

Class structure for all levels is essentially the same, with content based on appropriate developmental skills for the specific age. Each class begins with barre work. At the barre students learn correct posture and placement of the entire body. The exercises in this section also teach balance and promote strength. They include various types of jumps that are the building blocks for the allegro (jumping) steps of classical ballet.

Following the barre warm-up comes stretching. These gentle exercises progress in difficulty as students gain flexibility.

Essential concepts such as classroom etiquette, musical awareness, and spatial relationships to other dancers are taught early on. The components of the stage, theatre terminology and pantomime are explored during the year.

The last portion of class develops the locomotive skills that form the basis for all ballet steps that travel.

Students participate in a variety of free movement exercises that teach listening skills (responding to the tempo and mood of the music), levels (high, medium, and low), and directions (upstage, downstage, stage right and stage left).

Children's Programs

Our Early Childhood Program is specially designed to provide children between two and six years of age with a strong foundation in dance. It teaches young dancers the basic elements of movement. Space and how we relate to it. Both as an individual and as a member of a group. Musical rhythm, beat and time; opposites and directions; and some elementary positions of ballet. Integrated with these physical concepts are the intellectual concepts of self-awareness, creative expression, imagination, teamwork, and basic body/health awareness.Through participation in these classes, students also learn the basics of classroom behavior and manners. These concepts remain the same throughout their dancing years and include learning how to take turns, following another dancer or leading the way, waiting patiently, working together with others, and having a positive attitude.

Educators now know that training young children in the concepts of movement, sequencing, patterning, and spatial relationships lays a strong foundation for the later development of reading, mathematics, and other educational skills, all of this adds up to the discipline necessary to be an excellent student in school as well. Children in our Early Childhood Program work actively in all these areas.
